## TL;DR

We pre-train a 7B vision-language model on **18,000 hours of cooking YouTube**, then
fine-tune for robotic grasping with **no in-domain robot data**. It tops the GraspNet-1B
benchmark, beating prior SOTA that was trained on actual robot demos.

## The trick

Cooking videos are an *unreasonably* good source of object-interaction priors:
they're high-resolution, contain enormous diversity of containers/tools/foods,
and human hands serve as natural manipulation demonstrations. We co-train on
(frame, action description) pairs and learn a contact-prediction head from the
last 100ms of each grasp event.

## Numbers

- GraspNet-1B novel objects: **89.2%** (prior SOTA: 84.7%)
- Sim-to-real transfer with no real data: **77.1%** on the Franka Panda

## Limits

Tasks requiring force feedback (squeezing fruit, cracking eggs) remain hard. Vision
priors give the *what* and *where*; the *how-hard* signal is missing.

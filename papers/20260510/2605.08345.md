## TL;DR

We replace the long-range portion of self-attention with **streaming-compressed retrieval**:
old tokens are summarized into a fixed-size memory, and each new query retrieves a sparse
subset. Result: **10M effective context** with O(n) compute and no quality regression
on standard 128K-context benchmarks.

## Quick comparisons

|:-------|:----:|:--:|:---:|

## How we evaluate "10M context"

We curated a needle-in-a-haystack variant where the answer-evidence is split across
**3 distinct passages** dispersed in a 10M-token corpus. Recall@1 stays above 91%.

## Engineering notes

- Memory budget is configurable per layer; we found 8K compressed slots per layer
  works well.
- The retriever is trained with a small contrastive loss, jointly with the LLM.
- Cold-start the retriever from a strong base model — random init does not converge.

## TL;DR

We replace top-k softmax routing in a Mixture-of-Experts LLM with a tiny PPO-trained
router. The reward is **inference cost** + a quality regularizer. End result: at iso-quality,
the new router activates **3× fewer experts** per token.

## Why this is interesting

Top-k is a heuristic. It works, but nothing in the loss says "use as few experts as you
can get away with." Making cost an explicit reward signal turns out to be enough — and
the policy learns surprisingly smart fallbacks (e.g., always routing simple tokens like
punctuation through a single cheap expert).

## Results at a glance

| Setting | Active experts/token | MMLU | Speed |
|--------:|:--------------------:|:----:|:-----:|
| Mixtral-8x22B top-k=2 | 2 | 77.8 | 1.0× |
| Ours (PPO-router) | 0.66 (avg) | 77.9 | **3.1×** |

## Engineering caveats

The router has to be trained *after* the main MoE — joint training was unstable in our
hands. Plan for an extra fine-tune phase of ~3% of the original cost.

## TL;DR

We fit scaling laws to **DiT** (Diffusion Transformer) image models across 12 orders
of magnitude in training FLOPs — from 1e15 to 1e27. The relationship between loss and
compute is cleanly **linear in log-log space**, in contrast to the slightly sublinear
behavior reported for U-Net-based SD-XL-style models.

## Headline finding

At equal compute, DiT reaches FID **0.31 lower** than the best U-Net configuration,
*and* the gap widens with scale. The cost crossover point — where DiT becomes
strictly preferred — sits at around **2.3 × 10²¹ FLOPs**.

## Take-aways for practitioners

1. Stop training U-Nets above ~3B params. The compute-optimal frontier belongs to DiT.
2. Width-vs-depth ratios in DiT are far less sensitive than in LLMs.
3. Patch size matters more than expected — 2×2 wins decisively over 4×4 above 10²³ FLOPs.

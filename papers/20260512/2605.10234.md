## TL;DR

We curated **NOBEL-220**, a benchmark of 220 experimental procedures drawn from
Nobel-winning physics, chemistry, and medicine papers. Each task asks the model to
plan the experiment from scratch given only the research question and the era's
available techniques.

## Headline

A frontier 70B model **completes 41 tasks** end-to-end without hint scaffolding —
including a faithful reconstruction of Lavoisier's calorimeter protocol and a
working draft of CRISPR's "tracrRNA discovery" experimental plan.

## What this benchmark is *not*

It is **not** a replacement for empirical science. We measure whether the model can
*propose* the experimental design and key controls correctly — not whether it has
intuited a Nobel-class question itself. The questions are given.

## Common failure modes

- **Anachronism**: using a technique that wasn't yet invented (model loses points).
- **Missing controls**: very common in early chemistry tasks.
- **Sample-size hand-waving**: less of a problem post-2000.

## Why we built it

There is a real gap between LLM reasoning evals and "would this help an actual lab."
NOBEL-220 is one attempt to start closing it.

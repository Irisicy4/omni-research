## TL;DR

We pre-train a **3B-param world model** on raw YouTube video at 16fps, then condition a
much smaller video diffusion model on its latent rollouts. The result matches Sora-class
quality on the WAY-Bench eval while spending **~9.7× fewer GPU-hours**.

## Why it matters

The going assumption was that photoreal video generation requires direct end-to-end
diffusion training over hundreds of millions of clips. We show that **decoupling**
the physics prior from the rendering policy is dramatically cheaper, and even *improves*
temporal coherence on physics-heavy clips (pouring liquid, bouncing balls, cloth).

## Key results

- WAY-Bench overall: **74.3** (vs. Sora-2 73.1, Pika-3 71.8)
- Training compute: **3,240 H100-days** total (vs. ~31k for comparable baselines)
- Win-rate vs. Veo-2 on human preference: 53.4%

## Caveats

The world model only sees 8s of context; coherence degrades on long-horizon (>30s)
shots. We expect this to be straightforward to address by ramping context.
